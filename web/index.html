    Why this exists

    Most founder coverage is the company in a costume: the tidy arc, the rehearsed quote, the lesson stamped on at the end. We wanted the version that isn't ironed flat — the long middle where the person was still mostly a person and the company was mostly an idea.

    Every founder in the directory is a small research sandbox: dozens of articles, interviews, and transcripts, ingested and indexed so we can write — and you can search — against a real record instead of a press kit.

    How we write

    1. Talk to the person, not the press release.

      Every study begins with hours of recorded conversation. The company arrives later, and only as far as it explains the person.

    2. Cite everything we can.

      Where a date, number, or quote can be sourced, it is. Provenance lives in the footnotes — boring on purpose, and load-bearing.

    3. The subject sees the draft.

      They can't rewrite us, but they can correct us. We've killed pieces because the person was no longer comfortable.
